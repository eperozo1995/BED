How A Covid Housing Crisis Response Project Has Kept Hundreds From Eviction

By Eliana Perozo

In 2020, as isolation became the norm and folks all over Brooklyn were sent home, a new group of Crown Heights organizers, inspired by the 1930s eviction blockades, began to scheme. It wasn’t until an illegal eviction took place on Dean Street, and said organizers came together in an intense direct confrontation with the police, the hired muscle of the landlords, and the landlord themself that those select few realized it was time to really do something, thus Brooklyn Eviction Defense (BED) was born. As the pandemic led to severe economic loss for Brooklynites, community members all over turned to mutual aid and self organized efforts in order to do what the capitalist state was failing to do, provide. While many self organized coalitions have dissolved over the years, BED, a fully volunteer run organization, continues on.

BED has exhausted an extensive list of support efforts as they seek to address the multiple needs tenants have by data scraping, door knocking, radical direct action, and so forth. Recently, BED held a public housing defense at 964 Park Place which led to an influx of new organizers joining the organization. That housing defense required donation efforts, around the clock bodies and protection at 964 Park Place, political education, social media support, and much more. Through these efforts BED has been able to help form 24 tenant associations and three tenant councils and keep hundreds of tenants from being illegally evicted.
